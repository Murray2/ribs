Harry Ainlay Kitchen Ribs

Curricular Outcomes:

  • Grilling
  • Braising
  • Wet and Dry Marinades
  • Seasoning
  • Blanching
  • Saute

Ingredients:

Spice Rub

  • 3/4 cup Brown Sugar
  • 2/3 cup Coarse Salt
  • 1 tbsp Black Pepper
  • 2 tbsp Smoked Paprika
  • 2 tbsp Onion Powder
  • 3 tbsp Garlic Powder
  • 1 tbsp Cumin
  • 1 tbsp Mustard Powder
  • 2 tbsp Oregano
  • 2 tsp Cayenne Pepper
  • 2.25 oz coffee grounds

Ribs

  • Pork back ribs
  • BBQ rub
  • Canola oil
  • Apple cider vinegar
  • Braising liquid
  • BBQ sauce

Corn

  • Corn on the Cob
  • Sea Salt
  • Table Salt
  • Butter

Braised Greens

  • Braising Greens
  • Shallots
  • Canola Oil
  • Red Wine Vinegar
  • Heavy Cream

Procedure:

The Day Before:

Clean silver skin off the back of the pork ribs using a butter knife.

Combine all ingredients for the spice rub in a bowl and mix thoroughly, use gloves to break up any clumps of brown sugar, salt, or coffee

Mix 3 parts BBQ rub with 1 part canola oil and 1 part apple cider vinegar to make a wet rub. Liberally rub the ribs hard with the wet rub, marinade overnight.

The Day Of:

For the corn (or most vegetables), we want to use big pot blanching techniques. So the trick is to heavily salt the water, so it tastes like the ocean. Also, you want to have enough water, so you do not loose the rolling boil. When they come out, rub them with butter and sprinkle them with sea salt.

For braised greens, get your oil hot and add the shallots.

Add your braised greens as soon as the shallots are cooked. Use some water and red wine vinegar to deglaze the pan. Then after it reduces au sec, add your cream.

Make sure you readjust the seasoning for the cream.

Preheat convection oven to 375Â°F and preheat grill to medium high heat. Grill both sides of the ribs to achieve some color and flavour, and place into a pan.

Fill half way up with braising liquid and tinfoil the tops of the pan to cover. Place into a preheated convection oven, and cook till tender. About 2 hours, do not overcook to the point where the meat just falls apart.

Once tender, carefully transfer ribs to baking sheets with parchment paper. Save braising liquid for next time. Carefully brush your ribs with a homemade (or your favourite) BBQ sauce.

Continue to sauce the ribs every 15-30 minutes. Every time the sauce becomes solid and tacky, paint on another layer. This is what the end result will look like.

I always like to serve ribs with beans, greens, and corn. Give the food a picnic feeling.
